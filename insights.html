Key Insights

The data revealed patterns that should fundamentally shape how Stronghold communicates, engages supporters, and grows. These insights inform every recommendation in the roadmap.

Who Your Supporters Are: 5 Supporter Personas

Analysis of donation patterns and survey responses reveals five distinct supporter types. Each requires different messaging and engagement strategies.

Effectiveness Seekers

Motivated by proven impact and financial transparency. Want to know exactly where money goes.

What This Means: Lead with specific impact metrics and expense breakdowns.

Military Connection

Veterans, active duty, or military families. Drawn to operator credibility and mission alignment.

What This Means: Ephraim's SEAL background is a trust multiplier for this segment.

Monthly Community

Identity-driven givers who feel belonging through sustained support. Retention-sensitive.

What This Means: Recognition programs and insider content drive long-term loyalty.

Crisis Responders

Activated by specific events or news coverage. High acquisition, lower natural retention.

What This Means: Fast onboarding sequences are critical to convert impulse to commitment.

Tax Optimizers

Giving decisions tied to tax planning. Responsive to year-end appeals and DAF options.

What This Means: Q4 major gift campaigns should target this segment specifically.

The Language Shift: Supporter-Centric Messaging

This is the single most important messaging change. The current framing puts Stronghold at the center. The data shows supporters want to feel like they are the ones helping.

Current Framing

"Support what we're doing"

The supporter gives money to Stronghold. Stronghold does the work. The supporter is one step removed from the impact.

Recommended Framing

"I'm putting 3 med packs in Burma this month"

The supporter IS the one helping. They feel ownership of the outcome. They can tell friends exactly what their money does.

Why This Matters

Survey respondents struggled to explain Stronghold's model to friends. When asked what they would tell someone, many gave vague answers. Tangible impact language solves this.

Example: "$50/month = 3 med packs in the field" gives supporters a concrete story to tell. It transforms passive supporters into active advocates.

Voice and Tone Principles

The Brand Operating System analysis identified five core voice principles derived from supporter feedback and content performance. Every piece of content should pass through these filters.

1. Operator, Not Marketer

Write like a team leader briefing their people. Direct, specific, no filler. Every sentence has a job to do.

2. Show, Don't Summarize

Specific details over general claims. One rescued girl's story beats "we served 10,000 beneficiaries."

3. Humble Confidence

Stronghold does extraordinary things but never brags. Let the actions speak. Matter-of-fact about danger and sacrifice.

4. Invitation, Not Guilt

Never guilt-trip supporters. "Life is hard, and we never want to be a financial burden." Protect this absolutely.

5. Dignity Always

Those Stronghold serves are never victims, never statistics. They are families, communities, people with names and stories. This is non-negotiable.

The Visual Gap

A mission that saves lives deserves imagery that reflects that reality.

The current website relies heavily on custom illustrations. While well-executed, they create a disconnect: 85% of supporters said they want raw, authentic field content, but the website gives them polished illustrations.

Recommendation: Real photos from real operations should be the primary visual language. Illustration can support (infographics, maps), but never lead.

Email Strategy Gaps

  • No welcome sequence for new supporters
  • No "second donation" journey (the 30-90 day window is highest-leverage for retention)
  • No segmentation by supporter tier or tenure
  • No tracking of which emails drive donations
  • No A/B testing infrastructure

Sector evidence: A structured nurture sequence in the first 90 days can double retention rates.

The Retention Window

Supporter behavior data reveals a critical threshold: supporters who make it past 10 months are significantly more likely to stay long-term.

The 10-Month Mark

Supporters who make it past approximately 10 months show dramatically lower churn rates.

This suggests a specific intervention point: reach out to supporters at the 8-month mark with recognition, exclusive content, or a personal thank you.

See Roadmap Phase 1: Milestone Recognition and Re-engagement Flows directly address this finding.

Early Engagement is Decisive

Supporters with 7+ donations in their first 3 months show only 71% churn (vs 97.9% overall).

The first 90 days determine everything. This is where email sequences and impact reporting matter most.
