Key Finding

Retention is an engagement and relationship problem, not an affordability problem. Donors giving $15/month churn at roughly the same rate as donors giving $40/month. Industry benchmark: Average nonprofit donor retention is 40-45%. Communication quality matters more than donation amount.

The Problem

The right pages exist on the site: emergency medicine, humanitarian relief, refugee protection. They are well-linked internally.

But they don't rank because Google receives no external signal about what Stronghold is. Of all inbound link anchors:

  • 90.4% are brand name variations
  • Only 3.5% contain topical terms

Google knows Stronghold exists. It does not know what Stronghold does.

Comparable Organizations

Stronghold shares zero keywords with comparable organizations:

  • Project Dynamo
  • Free Burma Rangers
  • Team Rubicon

These organizations rank for the searches that should lead to Stronghold. We are simply not in the same search conversation.

What's Missing

  • FundraiseUp modal tracking is broken
  • No data on donation form open rates
  • No CRM for donor journey tracking
  • No lifecycle analytics
  • No cohort retention analysis
  • 50-70% of donation activity invisible

Form Abandonment: We do not have form abandonment data. The FundraiseUp donation modal is not tagged within GA4, which means we cannot track how many visitors open the form, begin filling it out, or at what point they leave. Fixing GA4 form tracking is a prerequisite for any meaningful conversion optimization work.

What GA4 Could Tell Us

  • User demographics and interests
  • Device and browser data
  • Geographic performance
  • Conversion paths and funnels
  • Session duration and engagement
  • Traffic source attribution

Why This Matters

Without attribution, it is impossible to:

  • Know which social media channels are converting
  • Know which email campaigns are converting
  • Calculate channel-level ROI
  • Identify which podcast appearances drove sign-ups
  • Let data drive content decisions

We are flying blind.
